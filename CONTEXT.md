# Kolmogorov Flow Data Assimilation

A research codebase for recovering the initial vorticity field of a chaotic 2D flow from sparse Lagrangian particle observations, using variational (gradient-based) optimization over a finite observation window.

## Language

### Flow & State

**Kolmogorov Flow**:
Forced 2D Navier-Stokes on a doubly-periodic domain with sinusoidal body forcing F = −n cos(ny) in vorticity form.
_Avoid_: KF (ambiguous in other contexts — write "Kolmogorov flow" in prose)

**Vorticity field** (`omega`):
The primary state variable of the Kolmogorov flow; a 2D scalar field whose Fourier coefficients (`omega_hat`) are the computational representation.
_Avoid_: velocity field, state vector (the state _is_ the vorticity field)

**Reynolds number** (`Re`):
Dimensionless parameter controlling flow viscosity and turbulence intensity; the key experimental axis (Re ∈ {40, 60, 100, 200} in current experiments).

**Forcing wavenumber** (`n`):
Integer wavenumber of the sinusoidal Kolmogorov forcing; fixed at n=4 in all current experiments.

**Attractor**:
The long-time invariant set of the chaotic Kolmogorov flow; represented computationally by the **attractor dataset**.
_Avoid_: "training data", "library"

**Attractor dataset**:
Large collection of `omega_hat` snapshots sampled at uniform intervals along a single long trajectory on the attractor; stored as `dataset.npy` under `Trjs/KF_datasets/Re=…/` and generated by `trj_generator.py`. Loaded memory-mapped with temporal subsampling controlled by `t_skip`.
_Avoid_: "library", "snapshot dataset"

**Attractor snapshot**:
One individual `omega_hat` state drawn from the **attractor dataset**; used either as a **true IC** or an **IC guess**.
_Avoid_: "sample", "data point"

**Attractor radius**:
RMS norm of attractor snapshots used to normalize distances between flow states; provides a characteristic scale for the flow's phase-space geometry.

### Data Assimilation

**Variational data assimilation** (also: 4D-Var):
The scientific task of this project — a deterministic, gradient-based inverse problem that recovers a point estimate of the **true IC** by minimizing an observation-mismatch loss over a finite **observation window**.
_Avoid_: "data assimilation" alone when precision matters (too broad); EnKF, particle filter (these are different DA paradigms)

**True IC** (`omega0_hat`):
The ground-truth vorticity field at t=0 that generated the observed particle trajectory; the target of reconstruction.
_Avoid_: "true initial condition" (verbose), "target state"

**IC guess** (`omega0_guess_hat`):
The optimizer's starting point, sampled from the **attractor** at a specified normalized distance from the **true IC**.
_Avoid_: "initial guess", "starting point" (use "IC guess" for precision)

**Observation window** (`T`):
The length of simulated time over which particle positions are observed; chosen near one Lyapunov time for each Re.
_Avoid_: "time horizon", "assimilation window"

**Number of observations** (`NT`):
The number of discrete time points within the observation window at which particle positions are recorded.

**Noisy observations** (`xp_traj_DA`, `yp_traj_DA` in code):
Particle positions corrupted by Gaussian noise (std `sigma_y` in y, `sigma_x = x__y_sigma * sigma_y` in x); the actual data fed to the optimizer. When `sigma_y=0` these equal the clean **target trajectory** positions.
_Avoid_: "DA observations", "corrupted positions" — and note that the `_DA` suffix in the variable names refers to "used for DA", not "produced by DA"

**Noisy velocity observations** (`up_traj_DA`, `vp_traj_DA` in code):
Inertial particle velocities corrupted by Gaussian noise (std `sigma_vy` in v_y, `sigma_vx = vx__vy_sigma * sigma_vy` in v_x); used to reset particle velocity at each observation step during the loss scan. Only generated when `St > 0` and `sigma_vy > 0`. When absent (`sigma_vy=0`), velocity resets use the clean **target trajectory** values.
_Avoid_: conflating with **noisy observations** (which are positions)

**Measurement mask** (`t_mask`):
Boolean array of length `int(T/dt)+1` marking which time steps carry an observation; controls which forward steps contribute to the loss.

### Particles

**Tracer particle**:
A Lagrangian particle that follows the fluid velocity exactly (Stokes number St=0, beta=0).
_Avoid_: "passive particle", "fluid particle"

**Inertial particle**:
A Lagrangian particle with its own dynamics driven by Stokes drag from the fluid (St > 0).
_Avoid_: "heavy particle"

**Stokes number** (`St`):
Dimensionless inertia parameter; St=0 gives **tracer particles**, St>0 gives **inertial particles**.

**Particle IC seed** (`PIC_seed`):
Random seed that determines the initial positions of particles within the periodic domain.
_Avoid_: "particle seed"

**True IC seed** (`TIC_seed`):
Random seed that selects which attractor snapshot becomes the **true IC** for a given experiment replicate.
_Avoid_: "IC seed" (ambiguous — there is also the IC guess seed)

### Optimization

**IC parametrization**:
The change of variables applied to `omega_hat` before optimization; controls which modes are optimized and conditions the problem.
Currently: **Fourier parametrization**.

**Fourier parametrization** (`Fourier_Param`):
IC parametrization that truncates the vorticity spectrum to wavenumbers |kx|, |ky| ≤ K (always K = NDOF//2) and applies spectral preconditioning P(k) = exp(−½ν β k²) to produce the **damped representation**; treated as a single indivisible operation.

**Damped representation** (`s` / `Z0` in code):
The optimizer's search variable — the packed real vector obtained by applying the inverse preconditioner P⁻¹ to the retained Fourier modes of `omega_hat`. Recovering `omega_hat` from `s` applies P, re-amplifying the high-wavenumber modes.
_Avoid_: "whitened variable", "s-space", "preconditioned IC"

**Attractor initialization** (AI):
Strategy for choosing the **IC guess** — samples uniformly from attractor snapshots whose normalized distance from the **true IC** falls within a specified band [min_norm, max_norm].

**L-BFGS**:
The standard optimizer for variational DA in this project — limited-memory BFGS with Armijo backtracking line search.
_Avoid_: BFGS, NCSR1, TN (these are removed or being removed)

**Joint optimization** (`Joint_Opt`):
Optimizer wrapper that alternates between L-BFGS steps on the vorticity **IC** (in **damped representation** space) and Newton steps on the particle state directly; used when observations are noisy and particle positions (and optionally velocities, for inertial particles) are themselves uncertain variables to be estimated. The particle state vector `PP_opt` is `[xp, yp]` for tracer particles and `[xp, yp, up, vp]` for inertial particles with velocity optimization.

**Loss function**:
Scalar objective minimized during variational DA; either **MSE-PP** or **MSE-Vel**.

**MSE-PP**:
Loss based on mean squared error of particle positions between the simulated and observed trajectories (periodic minimum-image distance).
_Avoid_: "position loss"

**MSE-Vel**:
Loss based on mean squared error of fluid velocity sampled at observed particle locations.
_Avoid_: "velocity loss"

**Adjoint solver**:
Reverse-mode differentiation through the coupled flow+particle trajectory to compute loss gradients and Hessian-vector products efficiently without storing the full forward trajectory.

**Largest Lyapunov exponent** (LLE):
The dominant exponential growth rate of infinitesimal perturbations to the Kolmogorov flow; used to set the **observation window** and to allocate mantissa bits in VP float experiments.

### Precision Studies

**VP float** (variable-precision float):
Custom floating-point format (C++ Pybind11 extension) with configurable mantissa and exponent bits; used to quantify how many mantissa bits the stored forward trajectory needs before gradient errors degrade optimizer convergence. All trajectory states are stored with the same mantissa bit-width (uniform allocation).
_Avoid_: "reduced precision", "low-precision float" (use "VP float" for this project's specific format)

### Experiment Structure

**DA case**:
One atomic unit of results — a single combination of true IC seed, particle IC seed, observation window, particle count, NT, IC guess, optimizer, loss function, and float precision. Corresponds to one row in `results.parquet` and one directory under `cases/`.
_Avoid_: "run", "trial", "experiment" (too vague)

**Opt init sweep**:
The set of **DA cases** that share the same (true IC, particle IC, T, n_part, NT, optimizer, loss, float precision) but vary the **IC guess** across `num_opt_inits` samples from the attractor.
_Avoid_: "multi-start", "ensemble run"

### Outcomes

**Reconstruction error**:
Normalized distance between the recovered IC (`omega0_DA_hat`) and the **true IC** (`omega0_hat`), normalized by the **attractor radius**; the primary metric for whether a **DA case** succeeded physically.
_Avoid_: "final error", "IC error" (use "reconstruction error" for precision)

**Reconstruction success**:
A **DA case** is considered successful when the **reconstruction error** falls below an acceptable threshold; distinct from **optimization success**.

**Optimization success**:
Whether the optimizer converged to a low-loss solution; necessary but not sufficient for **reconstruction success** — the optimizer can converge cleanly yet produce a poor reconstruction if observations are too sparse to constrain the true IC.
_Avoid_: conflating with reconstruction success

### Trajectories

**Target trajectory** (`target_trj`):
The forward simulation from the **true IC** — the ground-truth flow+particle evolution that the optimizer tries to match.
_Avoid_: "true trajectory", "observed trajectory"

**Reconstructed trajectory** (`DA_trj`):
The forward simulation from the recovered IC after optimization completes; compared against the **target trajectory** to assess reconstruction quality.
_Avoid_: "DA trajectory", "optimized trajectory"

**Initial trajectory** (`init_guess_trj`):
The forward simulation from the **IC guess** before any optimization; saved as a baseline for measuring how much the optimizer improved.
_Avoid_: "prior trajectory", "guess trajectory"

## Relationships

- The **attractor** is the source for both the **true IC** (via **true IC seed**) and the **IC guess** (via **attractor initialization**)
- One **true IC** generates one **target trajectory** of **tracer/inertial particles** over the **observation window**
- The **loss function** measures mismatch between a candidate trajectory (from the **IC guess**) and the observed particle positions
- The **adjoint solver** differentiates through the loss to provide gradients to the optimizer
- The **Fourier parametrization** maps the optimizer's search space → `omega_hat` before each forward simulation
- **NT** observations are placed within the **observation window** T according to the **measurement mask**
- The **attractor radius** normalizes the distance between the **true IC** and the **IC guess**

## Flagged ambiguities

- `xp_traj_DA` / `yp_traj_DA` in code means "particle positions used as DA input" (i.e., **noisy observations**), not "particle positions produced by DA." The `_DA` suffix in variable names is overloaded — in prose always say **noisy observations** for the input data and **reconstructed trajectory** for the optimizer output.

## Example dialogue

> **Dev:** "We're running a sweep over Re=100. Which **true IC** do we use?"
> **Researcher:** "We pick 5 different **true IC seeds** — each selects a different attractor snapshot as the **true IC**. For each one, we sample several **IC guesses** from the attractor using **attractor initialization** with min_norm=0.1."

> **Dev:** "Why does the **observation window** T depend on Re?"
> **Researcher:** "We set T to approximately one Lyapunov time — as Re increases, the **LLE** grows, so T shrinks. Too short and there's not enough information; too long and the problem becomes exponentially harder."

> **Dev:** "What's the difference between `PIC_seed` and `TIC_seed`?"
> **Researcher:** "The **true IC seed** picks which flow state we're trying to recover. The **particle IC seed** picks where the particles start — you can hold the flow fixed and try different particle configurations."
